AEO vs SEO: What's the Difference?

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking. AEO focuses on being the answer AI recommends.

By Visible2AI Team
December 12, 2025
10 min read

If you've spent any time in digital marketing, you know SEO — Search Engine Optimization. It's been the backbone of online visibility for over two decades. But there's a new acronym making waves: AEO, or Answer Engine Optimization.

The difference isn't just semantic. It represents a fundamental shift in how people find information online — and how businesses need to position themselves to be found.

Here's the bottom line: SEO helps you rank. AEO helps you get recommended.

What is SEO? (A Quick Refresher)

Search Engine Optimization is the practice of optimizing your website to rank higher in search engine results pages (SERPs) — primarily Google. The goal is to appear on page one when someone searches for keywords related to your business.

Traditional SEO focuses on:

  • Keyword optimization: Including target keywords in titles, headers, and content
  • Backlink building: Earning links from other websites to signal authority
  • Technical optimization: Ensuring fast load times, mobile responsiveness, and crawlability
  • Content creation: Publishing high-quality content that matches search intent
  • On-page optimization: Meta descriptions, alt text, URL structure, and internal linking

SEO is a mature discipline with well-established best practices. Tools like Semrush, Ahrefs, and Moz have made it accessible to businesses of all sizes. And it still matters — Google processes over 8.5 billion searches per day.

What is AEO? (The New Frontier)

Answer Engine Optimization is the practice of optimizing your content to be surfaced and recommended by AI-powered answer engines — tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google's AI Overviews, and Microsoft Copilot.

Unlike traditional search engines that return a list of links, answer engines provide direct responses to questions. They synthesize information from multiple sources and present a single, authoritative answer — often without requiring the user to click through to any website.

AEO focuses on:

  • Being the source AI cites: Getting mentioned and linked in AI-generated responses
  • Entity recognition: Ensuring AI systems understand who you are as a business
  • Structured data: Using schema markup so AI can parse your content accurately
  • Conversational content: Writing in Q&A format that mirrors how people ask questions
  • Topical authority: Demonstrating deep expertise that AI systems trust
  • Third-party validation: Building citations on external platforms that AI systems reference

SEO vs AEO: Key Differences

Factor SEO AEO
Goal Rank on page 1 of search results Be recommended in AI responses
User Experience User clicks link, visits your site User gets answer directly, may not visit site
Content Format Long-form, keyword-optimized pages Q&A format, concise answers, structured data
Primary Signal Backlinks and domain authority Entity recognition and topical authority
Technical Focus Page speed, mobile-first, crawlability Schema markup, structured data, JSON-LD
Competition 10 organic spots on page 1 Often only 3-5 recommendations given
Measurement Rankings, traffic, click-through rate AI mentions, citations, recommendation rate

Do You Need Both SEO and AEO?

Yes. But the emphasis depends on your audience and industry.

SEO isn't going away. Google still dominates search, and organic traffic remains valuable. But if you're only optimizing for traditional search, you're missing a rapidly growing channel.

Think of it this way:

  1. SEO gets you found when someone searches for solutions
  2. AEO gets you recommended when someone asks for advice

The good news? Many AEO best practices also improve your SEO. Structured data, comprehensive FAQ content, clear entity definitions, and topical authority all benefit both channels.

How to Get Started with AEO

Ready to optimize for AI visibility? Here are the key steps:

  1. Audit your current AI visibility Before you can improve, you need to know where you stand. Ask ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity questions your customers might ask. Are you being recommended? What competitors show up instead?
  2. Implement schema markup Add Organization, FAQPage, Product, and other relevant schema types. This structured data helps AI systems understand your content accurately.
  3. Create FAQ content Answer the questions your customers actually ask. Use natural, conversational language. Structure content in clear Q&A format.
  4. Build entity recognition Get listed on industry directories, build Wikipedia-style authority, earn press coverage, and establish consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across the web.
  5. Monitor and iterate Track your AI visibility over time. Test different content formats. Analyze which competitors are winning and why.

The Bottom Line

SEO and AEO aren't competing strategies — they're complementary. SEO helps you rank in search results. AEO helps you become the answer AI recommends.

As AI assistants become more prevalent in how people research and make decisions, AEO will only grow in importance. The businesses that start optimizing now will have a significant advantage over those who wait.

Don't just rank. Get recommended.
